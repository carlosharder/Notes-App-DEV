Yes. When calculating the benefits for Social Security, they assume that you will work until you retire and that you will continue to earn as much as you did for the last reported year.
Your benefits are figured off your top 35 years of earnings (in constant dollars). If you retire at 58, then from 59 until you start collecting, you won’t be racking up high earning years.
Now, this may not impact you much, as you get 90% of the average of the best 35 years month salary, but only up to some pretty low amount. Then, you get 32% of the next amount up to a fairly high amount and then finally you get only 15% of anything over that amount. Then, if you’re collecting at 62, you’ll only get 75% of that amount.
Since you’re thinking of retiring early, chance are you’re near the top end and thus the reduction will not hit you as hard.
